The financial sector was one of the first to adopt artificial intelligence at scale. Today, AI is fundamental for market prediction, risk management, and fraud detection.

## Market prediction

Machine learning models analyze millions of historical data points, news, social media sentiment, and macroeconomic indicators to predict market movements. Transformers and LSTMs are the most commonly used architectures.

Although no model can predict the market with certainty, AI systems can identify patterns and correlations that humans cannot see, providing a statistical advantage.

## Algorithmic trading

Trading algorithms execute operations in milliseconds based on market signals. AI enables more sophisticated strategies that adapt to changing market conditions in real-time.

It is estimated that over 70% of trades on major markets are executed by algorithms.

## Fraud detection

AI systems analyze transactions in real-time to identify suspicious patterns. They detect fraud that rule-based systems miss, reducing false positives and improving security.

## Automated financial advisory

Robo-advisors use AI to create and manage personalized investment portfolios based on each client's risk profile and goals. They manage millions of dollars at significantly lower costs than human advisors.

## Risks and regulation

AI in finance requires careful risk management. Models can fail in extreme market conditions, and the lack of transparency in algorithms poses regulatory challenges.
