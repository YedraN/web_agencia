## The 2026 freelance market

Freelance development has matured. Platforms, AI, and standardized processes make hiring a freelance developer more accessible than ever. But there's also more noise and more risk.

## Where to find talent

Upwork and Toptal for general profiles. Arc.dev for verified senior developers. LinkedIn for direct outreach. Twitter/X and GitHub for finding talent by seeing their work. Communities like Indie Hackers.

## How to evaluate a developer

Don't marry the CV. Give a short, paid test (4-8 hours) that simulates real work. Evaluate communication, code quality, deadline compliance, and ability to ask questions when things are unclear.

## Red flags in interviews

Promising impossible results in unrealistic timelines. Not asking questions about the project. Lack of portfolio or public projects. No experience with similar technologies. Suspiciously low prices.

## Project management for freelancers

Write a detailed brief. Use GitHub for code and issue tracking. Weekly sync meetings. Pay by milestones, not hours. Have a clear contract with intellectual property rights.

## Building long-term relationships

The best freelance developers aren't on open platforms. Nurture the relationship, pay well and on time, give constructive feedback. A freelancer who knows your codebase is gold.
