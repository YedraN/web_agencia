## What is a micro-SaaS

A small SaaS product focused on a specific niche, usually built and managed by one person or a very small team. Typical revenue: $1,000-20,000/month. Low competition, high profitability.

## Why now is the best time

Modern tools (no-code, AI, cloud) drastically reduce the cost of building and operating a SaaS. You can launch in weeks with near-zero investment. Niche is the new mainstream.

## Profitable niches for micro-SaaS

Tools for underserved industries: accounting firms, dental clinics, auto repair shops, restaurants, gyms. Integrations for popular software (Slack, Shopify, Notion). Automation for manual processes.

## Niche strategy

Find a group of professionals with a specific problem. Offer a simple solution that does one thing very well. Charge $10-50/month. 100 clients at $30/month is $36,000/year for one person.

## How to find ideas

Work in an industry for a while (observe problems). Talk to professionals in non-tech sectors. Read niche forums and communities. Ask "what takes the most of your time each week?"

## Marketing for micro-SaaS

You don't need mass marketing. Local SEO, niche communities, partnerships, very specific content. A single well-ranked article for a niche keyword can bring clients for years.

## The ceiling and how to break it

Limit: $20-30k/month as a solo operator. To scale: automate, outsource, or turn into a multi-niche product. Many successful micro-SaaS end up being acquired by larger companies for 3-5x annual revenue.
