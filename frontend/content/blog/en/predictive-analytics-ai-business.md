Predictive analytics with artificial intelligence is transforming business decision-making. It enables moving from understanding what happened (descriptive analytics) to predicting what will happen and acting on it.

## What is predictive analytics?

It is the use of data, statistical algorithms, and machine learning to identify the likelihood of future outcomes. Unlike traditional reports that look backward, predictive analytics looks forward.

## Main techniques

**Regression**: Predicts continuous numerical values (next month's sales, optimal price).

**Classification**: Predicts categories (customer about to churn, fraudulent transaction).

**Time series**: Predicts future values based on historical patterns (demand, web traffic).

**Survival models**: Predicts time until an event (when a customer will churn).

## Business applications

**Sales**: Revenue prediction, identification of highest purchase probability customers.

**Marketing**: Campaign response prediction, customer lifetime value, churn prediction.

**Operations**: Demand prediction for inventory, staff optimization.

**Finance**: Cash flow prediction, fraud detection, credit risk.

**HR**: Employee turnover prediction, high-potential talent identification.

## Implementation

1. Define the business problem you want to predict
2. Collect relevant historical data
3. Select and train the appropriate model
4. Validate model accuracy with test data
5. Deploy model to production
6. Integrate predictions into decision processes

## Tools

**Cloud platforms**: AWS SageMaker, Google Vertex AI, Azure ML.

**BI tools with ML**: Power BI, Tableau, Looker with ML integrations.

**Low-code platforms**: Obviously AI, Akkio, DataRobot (no coding required).

## Common mistakes

Overfitting (model too tuned to past data), ignoring business context, not updating models periodically, and confusing correlation with causation.
