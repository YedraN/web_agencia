## Why you need a technical co-founder

If you're not a developer, you need someone to build the product. A committed technical co-founder is better than any external agency or freelance developer in the long run.

## Where to look

The best places are active communities: Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, GitHub (contribute to projects), tech meetups, and networks like Y Combinator's Co-founder Matching platform.

## What to offer

"The idea" is not enough. Offer fair equity (30-50% for the technical co-founder), clear vision, initial traction or potential clients. Show that you bring value too.

## How to evaluate

Work on a small project together before committing. Evaluate communication, execution speed, and value alignment. Technical skill can improve; attitude cannot.

## Minimum legal agreement

Draft a 4-year vesting with a 1-year cliff. Define clear roles and an exit mechanism. Use platforms like Clerky or Stripe Atlas for the documents.
