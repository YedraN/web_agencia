For businesses that serve specific geographic areas, local SEO is the most important digital marketing channel. When someone searches "plumber near me" or "best coffee shop in Austin," local SEO determines whether you appear.

## Google Business Profile Optimization

Your Google Business Profile is the single most important local SEO asset. Claim and verify your listing. Complete every field — name, address, phone, categories, attributes, hours, and services. Add high-quality photos regularly. Post updates and offers. Respond to every review, positive and negative, professionally and promptly.

## Local Citations and NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone number. Your NAP must be identical across every directory — Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, Bing Places, industry-specific sites, and local chamber of commerce pages. Inconsistent NAP data confuses search engines and damages local rankings.

## Local Link Building

Earn backlinks from local sources: sponsoring community events, partnering with other local businesses, getting featured in local news, and joining the local chamber of commerce. Local links signal geographic relevance to search engines.

## Reviews as a Ranking Signal

Review quantity, velocity, and sentiment all influence local rankings. Encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews on Google and industry-specific platforms. Never buy fake reviews — Google penalizes this aggressively.

## Local Content Creation

Create content targeting local keywords: "best [service] in [city]," "guide to [city] [industry]," and "things to do in [neighborhood]." Location pages for each service area provide additional relevance signals.
