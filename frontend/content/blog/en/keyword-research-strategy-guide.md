Keyword research is the foundation of every successful SEO campaign. Without understanding what your audience searches for, you cannot create content that meets their needs or ranks in search results.

## Search Intent Is Everything

Keywords fall into four intent categories. **Informational** — the user wants to learn ("how to fix a leaky faucet"). **Navigational** — the user wants to find a specific site ("Facebook login"). **Commercial** — the user is researching before purchase ("best running shoes 2025"). **Transactional** — the user is ready to buy ("buy Nike Air Max online"). Align your content with the intent behind the keyword.

## Seed Keywords and Expansion

Start with seed keywords — broad terms describing your business, products, or topics. Use tools like Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, Semrush, or Ubersuggest to expand into related terms. Pay attention to **search volume**, **keyword difficulty**, and **cost-per-click** data.

## Long-Tail Keywords

Long-tail keywords (three or more words) have lower search volume but significantly higher conversion rates. A visitor searching "buy organic coffee beans Seattle" is much closer to purchasing than someone searching "coffee." Long-tail keywords also face less competition, making them achievable ranking targets.

## Competitor Analysis

Analyze which keywords your competitors rank for. Tools can reveal their top organic pages and the queries driving traffic. Identify gaps — keywords relevant to your business that competitors have overlooked — and prioritize those.

## Keyword Organization

Group keywords by topic and intent. Build content clusters around themes rather than targeting individual keywords in isolation. A well-organized keyword strategy prevents cannibalization and builds topical authority.

## Ongoing Refinement

Keyword research is not a one-time task. Search trends shift, competitors enter new spaces, and your business evolves. Revisit your keyword strategy quarterly to capture emerging opportunities and retire underperforming targets.
