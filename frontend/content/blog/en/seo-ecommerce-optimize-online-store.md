E-commerce SEO presents unique challenges. Thousands of product pages, duplicate content from manufacturers, complex site architecture, and fierce competition all complicate optimization. A systematic approach is essential.

## Site Architecture and Crawlability

Structure your store with a logical hierarchy: Homepage > Category > Subcategory > Product. Use breadcrumb navigation to reinforce structure and help users orient themselves. Ensure your robots.txt and XML sitemap guide crawlers efficiently. Avoid orphan pages — every product should be reachable through navigation.

## Product Page Optimization

Each product page needs a unique title tag and meta description that includes the product name and key attributes. Write original product descriptions — manufacturer copy creates duplicate content issues. Include high-quality images with descriptive alt text. Customer reviews add fresh content and social proof.

## Category Page Strategy

Category pages often rank better than individual product pages because they aggregate relevance signals. Write unique category descriptions (not just product lists). Optimize category titles for broader search terms while product pages target specific queries.

## Technical Considerations

**Page speed** is critical for e-commerce. Google found that a one-second delay in mobile load time can reduce conversions by up to 20%. Implement **structured data** (Product schema) to enable rich results with price, availability, and review stars. Use **canonical tags** to handle duplicate product variations (size, color).

## Managing Duplicate Content

E-commerce sites naturally generate duplicate content: same product across multiple URLs for different variations, filter pages, and session IDs. Use canonical tags, parameter handling in GSC, and noindex for low-value filter pages.

## Link Building for Product Pages

Product pages rarely earn natural links. Build links to content pages (guides, buying advice, comparison articles) that support the purchase journey and link to relevant products internally.
