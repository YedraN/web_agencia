A brand style guide is the definitive reference document for your visual and verbal identity. It ensures that everyone — from designers to marketers to external partners — applies your brand consistently across every touchpoint.

## What Is a Brand Style Guide?

A brand style guide (or brand guidelines document) codifies the rules for using your brand assets. It covers logo usage, color palette, typography, imagery, voice and tone, and more. Think of it as the instruction manual for your brand.

## Core Visual Elements

**Logo specifications** should include clear space requirements, minimum sizes, incorrect usage examples, and variations for different backgrounds. Your **color palette** must list primary, secondary, and accent colors with HEX, RGB, CMYK, and Pantone values. **Typography** guidelines define heading and body fonts, fallback stacks, and hierarchy rules.

## Voice and Tone

Define your brand personality traits and how they translate into writing. Include examples of preferred and discouraged phrasing. This section is crucial for content creators, copywriters, and customer-facing teams.

## Imagery and Iconography

Specify the style of photography, illustration, and iconography. Do you use flat illustrations or realistic photography? What about filters, overlays, or graphic treatments? Consistency here dramatically impacts brand recognition.

## Practical Applications

Include real-world examples: business cards, social media templates, email signatures, advertisement layouts, and digital mockups. Showing how guidelines apply in context reduces ambiguity.
