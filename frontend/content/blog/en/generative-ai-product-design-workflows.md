Generative AI is fundamentally changing how digital products are designed. From initial research to development handoff, AI can accelerate and improve every phase of the process.

## Research and definition

LLMs can analyze user interviews, identify patterns, and generate insights. They can also create synthetic personas based on market data and help define design problems and opportunities.

Tools like Dovetail and Condens use AI to analyze qualitative research and extract themes and patterns automatically.

## Ideation and conceptualization

Midjourney v7, DALL-E 4, and Stable Diffusion 4 allow generating dozens of visual concepts in minutes. Designers can explore creative directions much faster than with manual moodboards.

## Wireframing and prototyping

Galileo AI and Uizard convert text descriptions into wireframes and navigable prototypes. Designers can iterate on structure and flow without waiting for visual design.

## Visual design

AI can generate complete design systems, component variants, and graphic assets. Tools like Figma with AI plugins allow generating icons, illustrations, and patterns.

## Usability testing

Platforms like UserTesting with AI can analyze testing sessions, identify usability issues, and generate automated reports. AI can also simulate user interactions to detect early problems.

## The integrated workflow

1. AI-assisted research
2. Ideation and concepts with generative AI
3. AI-generated wireframes
4. Visual design with AI components
5. Functional prototyping with v0.dev or Bolt.new
6. Testing with AI analysis
7. Handoff with AI-generated documentation

## The designer's role

AI doesn't replace the designer, it automates repetitive tasks and frees time for strategic thinking, creativity, and high-level design decisions.
