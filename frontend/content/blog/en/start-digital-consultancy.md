## Why digital consulting?

Low barriers to entry, high demand, income from month one. If you have expertise in marketing, development, product, or digital strategy, you can monetize it as a consultant.

## Define your niche

"I don't know what to offer" is the #1 problem for new consultants. Specialize in an industry (fintech, healthtech), a service (growth, CRO, OKRs), or a client profile (seed startups, scale-ups).

## Build authority from day 1

Write on LinkedIn, publish on Medium, speak on podcasts, give webinars. Your authority is your main asset. A consultant without authority is a cheap hour seller.

## Your first client

Offer a free audit or an initial discount in exchange for a testimonial. The first case study is more valuable than the income. After 3 successful projects, you have traction for real rates.

## Pricing: hourly, project, or value

Hourly: easy but limits income. Project: better for both. Value: hard to sell but maximum return. Start with project, migrate to value when you have demonstrable results.

## Systems to scale

Don't be a consultant who sells their time. Create frameworks, methodologies, templates, and tools that multiply your impact. True scaling comes from selling packaged knowledge.

## From consultant to agency

At some point, you delegate delivery to focus on sales. Hire, train teams, standardize processes. Solo consulting has a ceiling; agencies don't.
